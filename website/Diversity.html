Who We Are

Here at Developing Diversity, we have a passion for data analysis, and we believe in the value of quantification. Often, visualizing exactly what a problem looks like is the push in the right direction that ultimately leads to a solution. And, after over a year of quarantine and job loss and economic turmoil, we know that the world is in dire need of solutions.

So, during the ExploreHacks 2021 Hackathon, we began developing a website with a data analysis software to quantify companies’ levels of diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI).
Here, we work with companies’ employee data to assess diversity and find hidden bias, and then we provide concrete tips on how to combat bias and increase DEI.
In doing this, we hope to create a sense of community and belonging within a workplace, so that no one will be undervalued or mistreated.


Why Do Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Matter?

Because two heads are better than one.
Because when an office, school, or movie set becomes an echo chamber for the white man, everyone else is drowned out.
Valuable ideas and creative solutions are muffled, and cries for help are silenced as they come.
Diversity matters because regardless of race, skin color, gender, sexual orientation, or financial status, everyone’s voices deserve to be heard.

But diversity alone isn't enough.

Having some minority employees or a few female administrators isn’t enough if their work isn’t being pushed forward or valued.
If these marginalized voices aren’t being amplified to the same volume as everyone else’s, then that’s not inclusion.
That’s tokenism––just a symbolic action that squeezes in some people from underrepresented groups, without truly valuing their contributions.

Inclusion is just as necessary as diversity

Inclusion is what actively allows for open dialogue and promotes creativity, making companies more representative and in-tune to their consumers’ needs.
In fact, it has been proven that diverse and inclusive companies do better!
When companies establish inclusive business cultures and policies, they are more likely to report 59.1% increases in creativity, innovation, and openness, and 37.9% better assessment of consumer interest and demand.
But with the undying systems of oppression in the United States that rob people of opportunities right under our noses, creating diversity and inclusion is much easier said than done.
It’s not just a one-time “Everyone is equally valued and considered at this company now!”
It’s not a rule that can be announced and then followed.

Diversity and Inclusion have to be built.

Systems that increase equity have to be created, enforced, and advertised to the people who need them.
They need to be flexible and change to fit the organization’s social climate, and they need to be modeled based on the requests of the people they are made for.


So,Start With Us

Reprogramming the culture of a workplace to become richer and more inclusive is no easy task, so our data analysis software helps companies clearly see the level of diversity, equity, and inclusion present within them.
After our algorithms quantify their levels of DEI, they can turn their attention to our Solutions tab.
There, we provide ways for companies to become more equitable, such as blind hiring methods and employee feedback systems.


However, even with all of our data, we stress that the benefits of DEI cannot be obtained if a company is just recruiting new employees for the sake of meeting a quota.
True DEI is intrinsically marked by the understanding that people of every background are valuable and should be appreciated.

Remember, change starts with you!
